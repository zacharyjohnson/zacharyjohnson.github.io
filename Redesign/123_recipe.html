Product Development, UX Design, UI Design

Overview

As a project for my ENTR 200 class, I was paired with a team of four other people to develop a viable product to develop a business around. We worked throughout the semester on four projects that guided us through the product and business development process. We could have developed any product into a business, but we decided to focus on a mobile recipe application.

Team

Our team was comprised of five people from unique majors including Mechanical Engineering, Management, Art and Design Studio, Aerospace Engineering, and Computer Science. Each person brought a unique perspective to the project that led to its success.

My Contributions

My main focus for the project was product development. I worked to ensure that our product provided a usable service to potential users. Through this, I conducted user interviews and research to refine our product idea, developed a minimum viable product, target market, wireframes, and final prototype mockups. Additionally, I organized weekly group meetings to ensure that the team stay focused on the project and we met our project milestones.

Problem

The class gave us incredible freedom for developing a product. This wide scope of possibilities made brainstorming daunting, so we narrowed our scope to a mobile application. After some brainstorming, we decided to tackle the cooking process as three of our four team members live in apartments and complained about never being able to find recipes that worked with the ingredients in their kitchen.

Wireframe

I next created wireframes. This allowed me to further think through unique aspects of the application and userflow. For example, I initially planned for the user to input their favorite types of food when creating an account, but as I began wireframing, I realized that is redundant and unnecessary since the MyKitchen ingredients they input on the next screen would dictate which recipes they can discover.

Prototype

Though the creating an app prototype was not a requirement for the class, I developed a prototype for the experience. Using the wireframes as a base, I first created a small style guide and then applied that guide to the various screens in the application.
