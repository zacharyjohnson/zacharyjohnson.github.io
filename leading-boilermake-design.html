Typically, our hackers are predominantly white, male, and upperclassmen. Many don‚Äôt stay for the entire event. As a team, we made it our goal to use our designs to invite a younger, more diverse group of hackers who want to stay and participate for the entire event.

My Goals as Team Lead

Previously, the design team did not operate as a design team. Different designers used different software, communication lacked, and it led to inconsistency and headaches for everyone involved. Using the skills I learned at my internship with Aetna Digital, I developed the following goals for the team:

  • Each designer uses the same software (Affinity Designer)
  • Implement 2-week design sprints
  • Hold regular in-person design critiques

Theme Brainstorming

After collecting all of this information, we began brainstorming a theme that encompassed the lessons we learned from our research.

We eventually settled on the idea of a choose your own adventure theme. It inherently has a fun, exciting feel to it based on the feelings people have associated with traveling, but also fits our goals of visually saying hackers can (1) create whatever they want, (2) learn along the way, (3) meet new friends, and, generally, have a fun adventure

Branding Attempt 1:¬†Peach Map

So. We wanted people to feel like they were choosing their own adventure. How do we represent that visually?¬†We sat took some time, and each designer spent some time sketching and mocking-up possible designs. After gathering these possible visuals, we had a design critique and sent what we colloquially called the "Peach Map"¬†to the BoilerMake Team for feedback.

The¬†Feedback

I'll spare you the time of reading the details of our feedback, but they were essentially this: nope.

Branding Attempt 2:¬†Game Map

After receiving that feedback, we went back to the drawing board. People didn't instantly recognize it as a map, the colors did not feel like an adventure, and it failed to convey the emotions we want people to feel while viewing our materials. So. We wanted folks to feel excited by the possibilities of the weekend, safe to attend and learn, and no intimidation by unnecessarily high technical skill bars. We realized that this fits with casual video games like Stardew Valley or Animal Crossing. We found some inspiration and started sketching out this idea, finally landing on our final design.

The¬†Feedback

This time, except for some minor aesthetic changes, the feedback was much more positive!¬†So we decided to move forward with this design.
